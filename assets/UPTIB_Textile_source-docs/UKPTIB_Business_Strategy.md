# 1. Executive Summary

**UK Pakistan Trade & Investment Board (UKPTIB)** is a London-based trade platform that exists to do one thing well: connect the two ends of the UK–Pakistan textile trade and capture the value that sits in the middle. Pakistan is one of the world’s leading textile-producing nations; the United Kingdom is one of Europe’s largest consumer markets for textiles and apparel. Between them flows over **US$2.27 billion** of goods a year, the overwhelming majority of it textiles. UKPTIB is built to organise, professionalise and grow that flow.

We operate **two complementary business lines** under a single brand:

- **Business Line 1 — Exporter Marketing & Market-Entry Services (Pakistan-facing). **We help Pakistani manufacturers — including capable producers who are exporting for the first time — become visible, credible and reachable to UK buyers. We package their products, tell their story, generate buyer interest, and walk them through the practical realities of selling into Britain.

- **Business Line 2 — Sourcing & Outsourcing Services (UK-facing). **We act as an outsourced sourcing house for UK businesses. A UK buyer tells us what they need — say, a thousand bed sheets to a defined specification — and we source it from vetted Pakistani manufacturers, control the quality, manage the timeline, handle export documentation, and deliver landed product to the UK.

**The strategic insight that unites the two is simple but powerful: each side feeds the other. **The supplier base we build and qualify through our marketing work becomes the ready-made, pre-vetted sourcing pool we draw on for UK buyers. The buyer demand we attract on the UK side becomes a concrete, revenue-generating reason for Pakistani exporters to join and stay with us. We are not running two businesses that happen to share a name — we are running a **two-sided trade platform** in which supply and demand are deliberately matched by us, and in which our knowledge of both ends is the moat.

We are starting where the opportunity is largest and the path is clearest: **Priority 1 — home textiles** (bed linen, hotel linen, towels and bath textiles), the single biggest category of Pakistani textile exports to the UK. From that base we extend into private-label apparel, sustainable and ethically-sourced textiles, hospitality and healthcare textiles, and sportswear and activewear — each a sector in which Pakistan already has proven manufacturing strength and the UK already shows strong, growing demand.

This document sets out the full picture: the market we are entering, the precise services we deliver on each side, the supplier network that is our core asset, the operating and quality-control model that makes the sourcing business credible, our revenue model, our phased go-to-market plan, the partnerships we will build, the targets we will hold ourselves to, the risks we must manage, and the long-term outcome we are working toward — to become **the recognised gateway for UK–Pakistan textile trade.**

# 2. About UK Pakistan Trade & Investment Board

## 2.1 Who we are

**UK Pakistan Trade & Investment Board (UKPTIB)**, based in London and operating online at [ukpaktrade.org.uk](https://ukpaktrade.org.uk), is a dedicated platform committed to strengthening bilateral trade, investment and business cooperation between the United Kingdom and Pakistan. Our focus, at least in this first chapter, is the sector where the two economies are already most deeply linked: textiles and apparel.

We sit deliberately between two communities that need each other but rarely connect efficiently. On one side are Pakistani manufacturers with genuine production capability, competitive pricing and, frequently, limited experience presenting themselves to international buyers or navigating UK market requirements. On the other side are UK retailers, wholesalers, importers, hospitality and healthcare buyers, e-commerce brands and entrepreneurs who want reliable, well-priced, ethically-produced textiles but lack the time, language, on-the-ground presence and quality-assurance capability to source directly from Pakistan with confidence. We exist to close that gap professionally and repeatedly.

## 2.2 Mission and vision

**Mission. **To position UK Pakistan Trade & Investment Board as the preferred gateway for Pakistani textile manufacturers seeking UK buyers, and for UK buyers seeking dependable Pakistani supply — facilitating meaningful, lasting commercial partnerships between both countries.

**Vision. **A mature, trusted UK–Pakistan textile trade platform with a deep roster of vetted Pakistani exporters on one side, a growing base of repeat UK buyers on the other, and UKPTIB recognised as the organisation that makes the connection safe, fast and high-quality.

## 2.3 The dual mandate

Everything we do falls under one of two mandates, and a great deal of what makes us distinctive comes from holding both at once:

- **Promote and represent Pakistani textile exporters** — give them visibility, credibility, market intelligence and direct routes to UK demand.

- **Source and de-risk Pakistani textiles for UK buyers** — find the right manufacturer, guarantee the specification, control the timeline and deliver landed product.

The chapters that follow treat each mandate in full — but Section 3 first explains why running them together is the heart of our strategy rather than an accident of scope.

# 3. The Strategic Thesis: One Platform, Two Markets

## 3.1 A two-sided model, by design

Most companies in this space pick one side. A marketing agency promotes manufacturers. A buying house sources for buyers. UKPTIB does both, on purpose, because the two activities share the same underlying asset — a deep, current, trusted knowledge of Pakistani textile supply and of UK textile demand — and because each activity makes the other stronger and cheaper to run.

## 3.2 How the two sides reinforce each other

The relationship between the two business lines is not merely additive; it is circular and compounding:

- **Marketing builds the sourcing pool. **Every Pakistani exporter we onboard, profile and qualify through our marketing service becomes a known, documented, pre-vetted supplier we can call on the moment a UK buyer places an order. Our supplier database is, in effect, a by-product of the marketing business that the sourcing business consumes for free.

- **Sourcing gives exporters a reason to join. **A directory listing is a promise; an actual UK purchase order is proof. Because we generate real buyer demand on the UK side, we can offer Pakistani exporters something most marketing platforms cannot — the genuine prospect of orders. That makes our membership proposition far easier to sell and far stickier to retain.

- **Each side improves our market intelligence. **Talking to UK buyers tells us exactly what specifications, certifications, price points and trends matter right now; talking to Pakistani manufacturers tells us what can actually be produced, at what minimum quantities and lead times. Holding both views makes us a better adviser to each.

- **Shared infrastructure, shared cost. **One brand, one website, one CRM, one events programme, one quality-assurance capability and one logistics network serve both lines. Fixed costs are spread across two revenue streams.

## 3.3 Our position in the value chain

We occupy the middle of the chain — the point of greatest friction and therefore greatest value. We do not own factories, and we do not need to. Our role is to organise, qualify, match, control and document. Concretely, depending on the engagement, we provide some or all of: discovery and matchmaking, sampling coordination, price negotiation, production monitoring, independent quality control, compliance and certification guidance, export documentation, and logistics coordination — the work that turns ‘a factory in Faisalabad’ and ‘a buyer in Birmingham’ into a completed, on-spec, on-time shipment.

# 4. The Market Opportunity

## 4.1 UK–Pakistan textile trade today

The United Kingdom remains one of Pakistan’s most important export destinations, and textiles dominate the relationship. In 2025 the UK imported approximately **US$2.27 billion** of goods from Pakistan, the large majority of it textile and apparel products. The breakdown below shows just how concentrated this trade is in the categories we serve.

| **UK imports from Pakistan (2025)**                 | **Approx. value**     | **Relevance to us**           |
| --------------------------------------------------- | --------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Knitwear & apparel (knit/crocheted)                 | £707 million          | Core apparel sourcing         |
| Home textiles, bed linen, towels & made-up articles | US$648 million        | Priority 1 — our launch focus |
| Woven apparel (not knit/crocheted)                  | US$367 million        | Private-label apparel         |
| Cotton & cotton products                            | US$40 million         | Raw material & fabric         |
| **Total goods imports from Pakistan**               | **≈ US$2.27 billion** | The pool we operate in        |

Official UK government trade data for the twelve months ending June 2025 reinforces the picture: clothing imports from Pakistan of roughly **£720 million** and textile-fabric imports of roughly **£520 million** — together more than **77% of all UK goods imports from Pakistan**. Within home textiles alone, UK imports of Pakistani bed linen, table linen, kitchen linen and bath products exceeded **US$597 million** in 2025. Textiles are not part of the UK–Pakistan trade relationship; they **_are_** the relationship.

## 4.2 The UK market by segment

Behind that trade flow sits one of Europe’s largest consumer markets. The segments most relevant to our priority sectors are large, growing, and structurally favourable to a well-organised low-cost-but-high-quality supplier base:

| **UK market segment**              | **Size / value**                     | **Trajectory**                   |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| Textile & apparel (overall)        | US$63.6 billion                      | Forecast toward US$100bn by 2034 |
| Clothing retail sales              | US$85 billion+ / yr                  | E-commerce-led growth            |
| Annual UK clothing expenditure     | £64.7 billion                        | Steady                           |
| Home textiles                      | US$7.2 billion                       | Toward US$10.5bn by 2033         |
| Bed & bath linen                   | US$4.9 billion                       | Steady growth                    |
| Sportswear & activewear            | ~US$6.78bn (£5.3bn); sector >£16.8bn | To US$9.36bn by 2034             |
| Medical / healthcare textiles      | ~US$1.84 billion                     | Growing on NHS demand            |
| Hospital linen supply & management | ~US$716m (2023)                      | To US$1.32bn by 2030 (>9%/yr)    |

Within home textiles, bed linen alone represents roughly 46% of the category — a market worth well over US$3 billion a year, and precisely where Pakistan is most competitive. This is why home textiles is our point of entry rather than a later phase.

## 4.3 Structural advantages that favour Pakistani supply

Three structural factors make Pakistani textiles genuinely attractive to UK buyers — not merely cheap, but a sound commercial choice:

- **Duty-free access (GSP+). **Pakistan’s preferential trade status gives qualifying Pakistani textiles duty-free entry into the UK, a direct and recurring cost advantage over many competing origins. This single fact is cited repeatedly across the supplier base and is central to the UK-facing pitch.

- **Cotton and capacity. **Pakistan is globally recognised for cotton quality, terry-towel manufacturing, large-scale production capacity and competitive pricing — the exact attributes that bulk UK buyers in bedding, towelling and basics care about most.

- **Specialised manufacturing hubs. **Faisalabad for home textiles and fabrics; Sialkot as one of the world’s leading hubs for sportswear, teamwear and customised athletic apparel; Lahore, Karachi and Multan for apparel, uniforms and institutional textiles. We can match a UK requirement to the right hub.

## 4.4 Demand trends shaping 2026–2030

UK buying behaviour is moving in directions that play to a curated, quality-controlled, ethically-positioned supply offer — which is exactly what UKPTIB intends to be:

- **Sustainable & ethical sourcing** is now the single biggest purchasing trend. Organic cotton, recycled polyester, sustainable packaging, transparent supply chains and certifications (GOTS, OEKO-TEX, BCI, Sedex, WRAP) are increasingly prerequisites, not differentiators.

- **Private-label manufacturing** is growing as UK brands outsource production and keep branding and marketing in-house — creating demand for partners offering design support, packaging, small runs and fast turnaround.

- **Premium home living** — luxury bedding, hotel-quality towels and lifestyle collections — continues to outperform basic commodity products.

- **E-commerce-driven sourcing** means a long tail of Amazon UK sellers, Shopify brands and D2C labels actively seeking reliable overseas manufacturing partners, often at lower volumes.

- **Recycled & circular textiles** are rising as UK and European buyers prepare for stricter sustainability regulation and favour suppliers with circular capability.

Each of these trends rewards exactly what we offer: a vetted, certification-aware, ethically-positioned supplier network presented and managed by a UK-based intermediary that speaks the buyer’s language.

# 6. Business Line 2 — Sourcing & Outsourcing Services (UK-Facing)

## 6.1 What this service is

This is the half of UKPTIB that faces the United Kingdom. We act as an outsourced sourcing house — effectively the Pakistan procurement department — for UK businesses that want to buy textiles from Pakistan but do not want to manage the complexity, distance and risk of doing it themselves.

The model is straightforward to describe and demanding to execute well. A UK buyer comes to us with a requirement — for example, a thousand bed sheets to a defined specification, quantity, quality level and target price. We identify the right Pakistani manufacturer from our vetted network, arrange and approve samples, negotiate price, place and monitor the order, run independent quality checks at the factory, manage export documentation, coordinate shipping, and deliver product that meets the agreed specification on the agreed timeline. The UK buyer deals with one accountable, English-speaking, UK-based partner; we handle everything that happens in Pakistan.

## 6.2 The UK buyer’s problem we solve

A UK retailer, wholesaler, hospitality buyer or e-commerce brand that wants Pakistani textiles directly typically runs into the following:

- They cannot easily identify which Pakistani factory is genuinely capable of, and reliable for, their specific product.

- They cannot verify quality from a distance, and a bad bulk order is expensive and slow to remedy.

- They have no one on the ground to monitor production, catch problems mid-run, and inspect goods before they ship.

- They find export documentation, compliance and logistics from Pakistan unfamiliar and time-consuming.

- They worry about ethical and certification compliance, which now carries real commercial and reputational weight.

Our entire UK-facing proposition is to absorb these risks: the buyer gets Pakistan’s cost and capability advantages without Pakistan’s sourcing risk, because we stand in the middle and are accountable for the outcome.

## 6.3 The end-to-end sourcing process

We run every UK order through a defined, repeatable process. The same backbone scales from a single trial order to an ongoing supply programme:

- **Brief & RFQ intake. **We capture the requirement in full — product, specification (e.g. fabric, thread count, GSM, sizes), quantities, quality level, certifications required, target price, packaging and delivery date.

- **Supplier identification & matching. **We match the brief to the right manufacturer(s) from our vetted network, drawing on our knowledge of who is genuinely strong in that product, at that volume, to that standard.

- **Sampling & approval. **We arrange samples from the most suitable factory, manage revisions, and secure the buyer’s written approval before any bulk commitment.

- **Price negotiation & purchase order. **We negotiate competitive pricing without compromising the agreed quality, confirm commercial terms, and place the order.

- **Production monitoring. **We track the critical path from yarn/fabric through to finishing, with midline inspection at roughly 40–50% of production to catch issues early, while there is still time to correct them.

- **Quality control & final audit. **We carry out pre-shipment inspection to the agreed AQL standard, auditing a high proportion of packed goods for construction, measurements and packaging. Nothing ships until it passes.

- **Export documentation. **We manage commercial invoices, packing lists, certificates of origin, GSP+ duty-free documentation and the rest of the paperwork that UK customs and the buyer require.

- **Logistics & shipping. **We coordinate freight (typically FOB Karachi or Port Qasim) with reliable partners and provide shipment visibility through to the UK.

- **Delivery & after-sales. **We confirm delivery against specification and handle any post-consignment issues, closing the loop and setting up the next order.

## 6.4 Quality assurance and compliance — our core promise

Quality control is not a step in the process; it is the product. The single most valuable thing we sell to a UK buyer is the confidence that what arrives matches what was ordered. Our quality model rests on:

- **Independent, on-the-ground inspection** at the factory, rather than reliance on the manufacturer’s own assurances.

- **Multi-stage checks** — raw-material and pre-production checks, in-line and midline inspection, and final pre-shipment audit to AQL standards.

- **Specification discipline** — GSM, thread count, colour-fastness, dimensional stability, stitching and packaging checked against the approved sample and the written brief.

- **Compliance and certification awareness** — working with factories that hold the relevant standards (GOTS, OEKO-TEX, BCI, Sedex, WRAP, ISO, and CE / EN ISO / AAMI where healthcare and workwear demand it), and guiding buyers and suppliers on what is required.

This is also where the two business lines connect most tangibly: the quality-assurance capability we build to serve UK buyers is the same capability that lets us credibly vet and recommend the Pakistani exporters in our marketing programme.

## 6.5 What we source

Our sourcing scope follows the same priority sectors as the marketing side, anchored in home textiles and extending outward. In practice we can source across:

- **Home & hospitality textiles** — bed linen, fitted and flat sheets, duvet covers and comforters, towels and bath linen, hotel linen, table and kitchen linen, mattress protectors and pillows.

- **Apparel** — private-label fashion basics, loungewear, children’s wear, denim and woven garments, knitwear and jersey.

- **Sportswear & activewear** — gymwear, leggings, performance apparel, teamwear and custom kit, particularly through the Sialkot manufacturing base.

- **Healthcare & institutional textiles** — scrubs, nurse and doctor uniforms, patient gowns, hospital bed linen, surgical and antimicrobial textiles, and care-home textiles.

- **Workwear & uniforms** — corporate, industrial, safety, school and security uniforms.

- **Yarn, fabric & made-ups** — greige and finished fabric, cotton and blended yarns, and a wide range of made-up textile articles.

**Why Pakistan: **duty-free GSP+ access, world-class cotton, large-scale capacity, recognised strength in towelling and bedding, specialised sportswear and uniform manufacturing, and competitive pricing.

**Why through UKPTIB: **because the cost and capability advantages of Pakistan are only worth having if the order actually arrives on-spec and on-time. ## 6.7 Winning UK buyers: a marketing-led approach

We do not simply wait for UK buyers to find us — we market to them with the same discipline we apply on the Pakistan side. Acquiring UK demand is an active marketing function in its own right, mirroring the engine in Section 5, and it is what keeps the sourcing business fed.

- **Outbound to UK decision-makers. **LinkedIn and email outreach to procurement, buying and sourcing leads at UK retailers, wholesalers, importers, hospitality groups, healthcare suppliers and e-commerce brands.

- **Demand-led content. **Messaging built around the things UK buyers actually weigh — cost, the GSP+ duty advantage, ethical sourcing, certifications and quality assurance — supported by case studies and supplier showcases that make sourcing from Pakistan an easy, low-risk decision.

- **Events as a buyer channel. **The UK–Pak Textile Export Forum and the monthly webinars double as buyer-acquisition occasions, putting vetted Pakistani supply directly in front of UK buyers.

- **Inbound capture. **Buyer-facing pages on ukpaktrade.org.uk with a simple ‘send us your brief / request a quote’ route that turns interest into live RFQs.

- **A trial-first offer. **A low-commitment first order (see the engagement models below) that lets a UK buyer test Pakistan and test us, designed to convert into repeat and programme sourcing.

**The symmetry is the point: both sides of the platform are marketed. **The Pakistan side is marketed to build supply; the UK side is marketed to build demand — and the two are brought together in our matchmaking.

## 6.8 Commercial terms and engagement models

We meet UK buyers where they are, from a cautious first trial to a standing programme:

- **Trial / single order** — a defined one-off sourcing project, ideal for a buyer testing Pakistan and testing us.

- **Repeat / programme sourcing** — ongoing supply against a rolling forecast, where we become the buyer’s effective Pakistan procurement function.

- **Quality-assurance-only** — for buyers who already have a factory but want our independent inspection and on-the-ground oversight.

Minimum order quantities, lead times and pricing vary by product and factory; across our network MOQs range from as low as 50–100 pieces with specialist makers to several hundred per size and colour for bulk bedding and towelling, with typical lead times in the order of two to four weeks for many made-up products. We match each buyer to a factory whose terms fit their volume.

## 7.3 Vetting and onboarding

A supplier does not simply appear in our pool; they earn a place in it. Our onboarding draws directly on the quality-assurance discipline described in Section 6.4:

- **Profiling. **We document the manufacturer’s products, capacity, fabrics, certifications, MOQs and lead times — the same exercise that produces their marketing profile.

- **Verification. **We assess capability and compliance, including the certifications that matter for the categories they serve.

- **Trial validation. **Where possible we validate through sampling and, ideally, an initial supervised order, so that recommendation rests on evidence rather than claims.

- **Ongoing performance tracking. **Suppliers we use are continuously assessed on quality, reliability and responsiveness, and our recommendations reflect that live record.

| **One asset, two businesses** Every hour spent profiling and vetting a Pakistani manufacturer produces both a marketing-ready company profile and a sourcing-ready supplier record. The work is done once and monetised twice. |

# 8. Operations and Delivery Model

UKPTIB runs on a small set of shared operational capabilities that serve both business lines. Keeping these centralised is what makes the dual model efficient rather than twice the work. At a glance, the two working processes — how we work with Pakistani exporters, and how we work with UK buyers — run in parallel and meet at the point of matchmaking:

_Figure 2 — How we work with each side: a five-step marketing journey with Pakistani exporters and a five-step sourcing journey with UK buyers, meeting where a UK requirement is matched to a vetted supplier._

## 8.1 Shared infrastructure

- **Brand & web presence **(ukpaktrade.org.uk) — the single front door for both Pakistani exporters and UK buyers, including the supplier directory and buyer-facing sourcing pages.

- **CRM & lead management **— one system tracking exporter members and UK buyers, the matches between them, and the status of live opportunities and orders.

- **Supplier knowledge base **— the structured, continuously updated record of the network described in Section 7.

- **Quality-assurance capability **— inspection and audit processes (and inspection partners on the ground in Pakistan) that underpin every sourcing order and every supplier recommendation.

- **Events programme **— the monthly webinars and quarterly export forum that generate exporter leads and double as buyer-engagement occasions.

- **Logistics & documentation **— freight coordination and export-documentation handling shared across all UK orders.

## 8.2 How an order flows through the organisation

On the sourcing side, a UK order flows through the nine-step process in Section 6.3, owned end-to-end by UKPTIB with the buyer dealing with a single point of contact. On the marketing side, an exporter flows through the three-phase engine in Section 5.6 and then into the supplier pool. The two flows meet in the CRM, where a UK requirement is matched to a profiled, vetted exporter — the moment the platform delivers its central value.

## 8.3 Team and capability build-out

The operating model implies a lean core team with clearly split responsibilities: UK-facing business development and account management; Pakistan-facing supplier relationships and onboarding; quality assurance and inspection coordination; marketing and events; and operations, documentation and logistics. Early on, several of these can be combined or supported by partners and contractors; they separate into distinct functions as volume grows.

# 9. Revenue Model

The dual mandate produces two distinct, complementary revenue streams — recurring fees from the Pakistan side and transaction-linked income from the UK side — which together smooth the business across the sales cycle.

## 9.1 Pakistan-side revenue (recurring)

- **Membership fees** across the Basic, Professional and Premium tiers — the recurring backbone of this stream.

- **Featured promotion and premium placement** — enhanced visibility within the directory, showcases and reports.

- **Event participation** — paid participation in forums and showcases for exporters who want a platform in front of buyers.

- **Managed services** — dedicated UK market-advisor support and buyer-sourcing campaigns at the Premium tier.

## 9.2 UK-side revenue (transaction-linked)

- **Sourcing commission / margin** on orders we source and deliver for UK buyers — the principal UK-side stream, scaling directly with order volume.

- **Quality-assurance & inspection fees** for independent inspection and on-the-ground oversight, including for quality-assurance-only engagements.

- **Programme / retainer arrangements** for buyers for whom we act as an ongoing Pakistan procurement function.

## 9.3 Blended economics

The two streams are intentionally counter-balanced. Membership income is predictable and recurring but modest per account; sourcing income is larger per transaction but lumpier. Run together they produce a steadier whole, and — crucially — they share a single cost base (Section 8.1), so the marginal cost of serving one more exporter or fulfilling one more order is low. As the supplier pool deepens and buyer relationships compound, the sourcing stream is expected to become the larger of the two, with memberships continuing to fund supplier acquisition and the platform’s visibility.

# 10. Go-to-Market and Implementation Roadmap

We phase the build so that early effort creates the assets later phases depend on — above all, a credible supplier pool and a first set of UK reference orders, both anchored in Priority 1 home textiles.

## 10.1 Phase 1 — Foundation (roughly months 1–3)

- Finalise positioning, brand and the ukpaktrade.org.uk presence, with clearly separated exporter and buyer journeys.

- Convert the existing knowledge base into a working, structured supplier directory; begin formal onboarding and vetting of Priority 1 home-textile manufacturers.

- Stand up the CRM, the quality-assurance process and inspection arrangements on the ground.

- Produce the “UK Textile Market Entry Guide 2026” lead magnet and launch Phase 1 awareness marketing (LinkedIn, Facebook, email).

## 10.2 Phase 2 — Activation (roughly months 3–6)

- Launch the membership tiers and begin converting exporter leads into paying members.

- Begin UK buyer outreach for home textiles and secure first trial sourcing orders — the reference cases that prove the model.

- Run the first monthly webinars and the inaugural UK–Pak Textile Export Forum.

- Execute and document the first end-to-end orders, capturing testimonials and case studies for both sides.

## 10.3 Phase 3 — Scale (roughly months 6–12)

- Deepen the supplier pool and extend into Priority 2–4 and the sportswear and healthcare extensions.

- Convert trial UK buyers into repeat and programme relationships; grow sourcing volume.

- Scale the marketing engine and the events programme; formalise strategic partnerships (Section 11).

- Build out the team functions described in Section 8.3 as volume justifies.

## 10.4 Beyond year one

With a proven model in textiles, the platform can broaden — additional textile sub-sectors, deeper UK-buyer programmes, and, in time, the wider trade-and-investment remit the organisation’s name implies. The textile business is the wedge; the gateway is the destination.

# 13. Risk Considerations and Mitigations

A sourcing-and-marketing intermediary lives or dies on trust and execution. The principal risks, and how the model manages them:

| **Risk**                              | **Mitigation**                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Quality failure on a bulk order       | Multi-stage independent inspection (raw material, midline at 40–50%, final pre-shipment to AQL); sample approval before bulk; supplier performance tracking |
| Supplier reliability / delivery delay | Vetting and trial validation before recommendation; production monitoring of the critical path; a deep enough pool to offer alternatives                    |
| Compliance & certification gaps       | Working with certified factories (GOTS, OEKO-TEX, BCI, Sedex, WRAP, ISO, CE/EN ISO/AAMI as relevant); guidance to buyers and suppliers on requirements      |
| Buyer concentration                   | Build a base of many UK buyers across sectors rather than dependence on a few; balance recurring membership income against transaction income               |
| Exporter churn                        | Convert membership value into real buyer conversations; lead with quick wins in Priority 1 to demonstrate ROI early                                         |
| Currency & cost movement              | Clear, current commercial terms per order; the structural GSP+ duty advantage cushions UK landed cost                                                       |
| Reputational risk in the middle       | Independent QC and accountability for outcomes; we win precisely by being the party that does not let either side down                                      |
