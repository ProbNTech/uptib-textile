**INTRODUCTION**

# The Gateway Between Pakistan and the UK Textile Market

_Connecting Pakistani textile manufacturers directly with buyers across the United Kingdom._

## Objective

To position the UK Pakistan Trade & Investment Board (UPTIB) as the preferred gateway for Pakistani textile manufacturers, exporters, apparel brands, home-textile producers and sourcing companies seeking access to UK buyers, distributors, retailers, wholesalers and e-commerce channels.

## Executive Summary

Pakistan is one of the world’s leading textile-exporting nations, with proven strength in home textiles, bed linen, towels, apparel and garments, denim, cotton fabrics and fashion accessories.

The United Kingdom continues to be a major destination for Pakistani textiles — driven by a large Pakistani diaspora, growing demand for ethical sourcing, duty-free trade benefits under GSP+, expanding private-label manufacturing, and continuous demand from retailers, wholesalers, hotels, healthcare and hospitality.

UPTIB connects Pakistani exporters directly with UK buyers through a structured exporter-acquisition programme — backed by hands-on support for market entry, compliance, networking and lead generation, and the UK warehousing and e-commerce needed to sell into Britain at scale. Our aim is to help Pakistani manufacturers grow from one-off suppliers into international brand owners selling across Britain and Europe.

## What UPTIB Does

- **Marketing & sales** — we make Pakistani manufacturers visible, credible and reachable to UK buyers, and generate the demand that turns into orders.

- **Sourcing & outsourcing** — as a buying house we also source Pakistani textiles for UK buyers, which means our exporters sit in a supplier pool we actively buy from on behalf of real clients.

- **E-commerce & warehousing** — we set you up on Amazon and store, pick, pack and ship from UK warehousing, close to your customers.

- **Logistics & GSP+** — we move the goods and handle the paperwork, with qualifying textiles entering the UK duty-free under GSP+.

## Why the United Kingdom

- One of Europe’s largest textile and apparel markets

- A large Pakistani diaspora and established trade ties

- Duty-free access for qualifying textiles under GSP+

- Rapidly growing demand for ethical and sustainable sourcing

- Expanding private-label and e-commerce opportunities

- Continuous demand from retail, hospitality and healthcare

## What UK Buyers Want — Trends 2026–2030

- **Sustainable & ethical sourcing** — the single biggest purchasing trend. Demand for organic cotton, recycled polyester, sustainable packaging, ethical manufacturing and transparent supply chains. Many retailers now require certifications (GOTS, OEKO-TEX, BCI, Sedex, WRAP, ISO) before onboarding.

- **Private-label manufacturing** — brands outsource production and focus on branding. Growing demand for design support, packaging, small runs, fast turnaround and product development.

- **Premium home living** — higher spend on luxury bedding, hotel-quality towels and lifestyle home collections.

- **E-commerce-driven sourcing** — Amazon UK sellers, Shopify brands and direct-to-consumer labels actively seeking reliable manufacturing partners.

- **Recycled & circular textiles** — recycled cotton and polyester and textile-to-textile recycling, ahead of stricter UK and EU sustainability regulation.

## Categories with the Strongest UK Demand

- **Bed linen & home textiles** — the largest home-textile category (~46% of the UK home-textile market, over US$ 3bn and rising toward ~US$ 10.5bn by 2033): bed sheets, duvet covers, pillow covers, hospitality and luxury cotton bedding.

- **Towels & bath linen** — a ~US$ 4.9bn market: luxury and hotel towels, spa and organic ranges, where Pakistan’s terry-towel capacity and cotton quality are world-class.

- **Apparel & garments** — a ~US$ 63.6bn market (forecast toward ~US$ 100bn by 2034): private-label, fashion basics, activewear, loungewear, workwear, children’s and sustainable apparel.
