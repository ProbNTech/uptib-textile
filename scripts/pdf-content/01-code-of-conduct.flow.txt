1. Introduction
The Code of Conduct for UK-Pakistan Tech Council is a foundational document established under the authority
of our organisation's Constitution. It outlines the principles and standards that all members are expected to
uphold in their professional and personal conduct.
As a binding document, adherence to this Code is mandatory for all members, ensuring consistency, integrity,
and professionalism in our collective endeavours. By adhering to these ethical and behavioural guidelines, we
demonstrate our commitment to excellence, integrity, and mutual respect within our community and in our
interactions with others.
All members are required to give an undertaking to the effect that they would abide by the UPTECH Code of
Conduct. The Code of Conduct also specifies the procedure for the action to be taken against concerned
members for any breach of this Code.

2. Core Principles
.

Integrity
Members will consistently demonstrate honesty and ethical behaviour in all actions and decisions.

.

Respect & Equality
Treat all members with dignity, equality, and cultural sensitivity, without discrimination based on any criteria.

.

Competence
Members will accurately represent their skills and experience and continuously strive to maintain professional
competence.

.

Loyalty
Act with unwavering loyalty towards the Council and maintain confidentiality when entrusted with sensitive
information.

.

Impartiality
Provide objective advice, disclosing any potential conflicts of interest promptly and transparently.

.

Ethical Conduct
Members will not pursue personal gain at the expense of the Council and actively work to enhance its reputation.

.

Non-discrimination
Refrain from engaging in discriminatory practices in any professional activities.

.

Continuous Development
Commit to lifelong learning, actively seeking opportunities to develop skills and knowledge, and encouraging
fellow members to do the same.

.

Professionalism
Adhere to recognised professional standards, offering services and advice diligently and responsibly within their
areas of expertise.

4. Disciplinary Procedure
All members of the Council are expected to adhere to the Code of Conduct. In the event of a member wishing to
file a complaint against other members for violating the Code, the following procedures apply:
4.1. Filing a Complaint
All complaints must be submitted in writing and sent to the Management Committee of the Council. Any member
is eligible to lodge a complaint.

4.2. Investigation
Upon receipt, the complaint will undergo investigation by a Committee vested with the authority to summon any
member(s) deemed relevant to the matter.

4.3. Right to Respond
Should the Committee determine potential misconduct, the implicated member(s) will be afforded a 7-day period
to respond to the allegations.

4.4. Disciplinary Actions
Should the member(s) be found guilty, disciplinary actions may include caution, reprimand, suspension, or
expulsion from Council membership.

4.5. Dismissal of Complaint
If the complaint lacks merit following the Committee’s evaluation, the complainant will be duly informed.

4.6. Fair Hearing
In instances where the matter is referred to the Disciplinary Committee, formal proceedings will be arranged for a
fair hearing of the charges.

4.7. Escalation
If the Committee finds the response inadequate or no written representation is received, the matter will be
escalated to the Disciplinary Committee.

5. Confidentiality of Proceedings
For caution and reprimand, the Council will disseminate details of the circumstances and outcome to all
members without disclosing the member’s identity. In cases of suspension or expulsion, the Council will, at its
discretion, inform members confidentially.

6. Right to Appeal
Affected members retain the right to appeal the committee’s decision within 7 days, submitting a written appeal.
The Executive Committee will then review the appeal. The verdict rendered by the Executive Committee on the
appeal(s) shall be final and non-negotiable.
